North Star — Question 1 of 4

Who is the one person you're writing this for?

In the Scribe Method, we don't write for "everyone." We write for one specific person. Picture them: what's their role, their situation, their struggle?

Scribe Method Tip: At Scribe, we spend an entire call defining your target reader. The more specific you are, the more powerful your book becomes. "Everyone" is not a reader — give us a name, a role, a pain point.

North Star — Question 2 of 4

What's your Champagne Moment?

Imagine your book is published and wildly successful. What single event happens that makes you crack open the champagne? Be specific — this becomes your North Star.

Scribe Method Tip: Your Champagne Moment is the single event you'd celebrate. It's not "selling copies" — it's something like "A CEO I admire emails me to say my book changed how they run their company." The more vivid, the better.

North Star — Question 3 of 4

What will your reader gain from this book?

Think about the person you described. After they finish your book, what changes for them? What can they do, believe, or understand that they couldn't before?

Scribe Method Tip: The best books create a transformation in the reader. Not just information — a shift in how they see the world. What's the "before and after" for your reader?

North Star — Question 4 of 4

What will you gain from this book?

Be honest — your book should serve you too. What doors do you want it to open? Select all that apply.
